Computational Applications to Policy and Strategy

CAPS is a short course on AI strategy and policy

Get the Syllabus View on GitHub

Computational Applications to Policy and Strategy (CAPS) is a short course that prepares students of international relations to critically evaluate AI algorithms, specifically for applications that challenge conventional modes of policy and strategic decision-making.

The course is taught as a professional development course at Johns Hopkins University's School of Advanced International Studies.

The course is set to start again in late October 2019. CAPS was previously taught, in different forms, at Johns Hopkins in the Spring 2018, Fall 2018 and Spring 2019 semesters. The course was delevoped and is taught by Leo Klenner, Henry Fung and Cory Combs.
