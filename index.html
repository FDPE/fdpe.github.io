Computational Applications to Policy and Strategy

CAPS is a short course on AI strategy and policy

Computational Applications to Policy and Strategy (CAPS) is a short course that prepares students of international relations to critically evaluate AI algorithms, specifically for applications that challenge conventional modes of policy and strategic decision-making.

CAPS is taught as a professional development course at Johns Hopkins University's School of Advanced International Studies (SAIS).

The course isd taught by Leo Klenner, Henry Fung, and Cory Combs, who developed CAPS while students at SAIS.

CAPS is set to start again in late October 2019. For any inquiries, please reach out via email.
